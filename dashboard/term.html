Risk Analysis by Loan Term

60 month loans carry significantly higher default risk than 36 month loans. Despite representing only 21% of portfolio count, 60 month terms drive 56% of expected losses. The 32.1% vs 15.5% default rate gap reflects extended exposure duration, borrower delinquency patterns over time, and higher average loan amounts amplifying loss severity.
