Methodology

Transparency regarding data sources, definitions, assumptions, and analytical approach.

Loss Assumptions

Parameter Value Rationale
Recovery Rate 70% Expected recovery on defaulted unsecured consumer credit. Consistent with published unsecured consumer credit recovery ranges (30 to 40% LGD) reported in academic and industry literature for personal loan portfolios.
Loss Given Default 30% Complement of recovery rate. Represents cash loss after collections efforts.
Default Definition 60+ days past due Industry standard for unsecured consumer credit. Loans 60+ days delinquent treated as defaulted.
Exposure Basis Current portfolio value Expected loss calculated on current outstanding balances. Does not account for principal paydown or prepayments.

Data Definitions

Loan Status Categories

  • Current: No payments missed or less than 30 days past due
  • Late (30 to 120 days): Between 30 and 120 days delinquent
  • Default: 60 or more days past due (outcome measure)
  • Fully Paid: Loan fully repaid (excluded from analysis)
  • Charged Off: Loan written off by lender (treated as default)

Portfolio Definition

  • Data source: LendingClub Accepted Loans 2007 to 2018 Q4
  • Population: 1,274,549 loans
  • Geographic: United States
  • Loan types: Personal, debt consolidation, home improvement, etc.
  • Excluded from performing balance calculations: fully paid loans (outcome observed). Charged-off loans are retained and classified as defaults in loss rate calculations.

Analytical Approach

Risk Segmentation

Portfolio segmented by loan grade (A-G), term (36 vs 60 months), purpose, DTI, employment length, and home ownership. Default rates calculated by segment. Expected loss allocated proportionally to segment risk and size.

Predictive Modeling

HistGradientBoosting regressor trained to predict interest rates from origination features. Model performance indicates degree to which rates reflect credit risk. Model residuals inform underpricing analysis.

Data Quality

Missing Data Handling

  • Columns with more than 50 percent null values: excluded
  • Columns with less than 5 percent null values: forward filled or interpolated
  • Categorical nulls (employment length, home ownership): mapped to sentinel value
  • Numeric nulls: median imputation by grade

Outlier Treatment

  • Annual income above 500k dollars: capped at 500k
  • Loan amounts above 100k dollars: kept as is (reflects actual portfolio)
  • DTI above 100 percent: flagged but retained
  • Interest rates outside 1-35 percent range: excluded (data quality issues)

Crisis Era Adjustment

Loans originated during 2008-2010 reflect stress period performance during the Great Recession. In this dashboard, these cohorts are flagged separately and excluded from the primary forward-looking baseline. This is a modeling choice used to separate stress-period behavior from normal-period estimates, and not a claim that crisis outcomes are uninformative.

Period Default Rate Interpretation
2007 (Pre Crisis) 12 to 14% Normal period baseline
2008 to 2010 (Crisis) 24 to 30% Elevated due to recession. Use with caution.
2011 onward (Post Crisis) 16 to 18% Recovery period. Approaching normal rates.

Rate Predictor Technical Specifications

Key Findings

  • Term length accounts for 27.7% of rate variation
  • FICO score contributes 24.2%
  • Loan amount drives 9.9% of variation
  • Purpose and home ownership add 6 to 7 percent each
  • Delinquency history and inquiry count are minor factors

Model Limitations

  • Cannot explain extreme rates (below 6% or above 25%)
  • Uses only origination features available at assessment
  • Assumes rate reflects credit risk pricing
  • Does not include lender subgrade scoring
  • Historical training data (2007 to 2018) only
