Rate Predictor

LendingClub's interest rate pricing is largely systematic and driven by a handful of identifiable factors. This analysis breaks down which borrower attributes most influence the rate a borrower receives, and what that means for investors.

Model context: Test R² is approximately 0.52. Notably, grade, subgrade, and installment amount were intentionally excluded. These are internal LendingClub assignments not available to external parties at origination. The model uses only borrower-observable inputs, so the R² reflects realistic external predictive power, not a ceiling.

What this means for investors

  • In this sample, rate is a useful proxy for credit risk. Borrowers assigned higher rates tend to default at higher rates
  • Loan term is one of the strongest drivers of rate. 60 month loans tend to carry higher rates
  • FICO score is among the largest drivers, consistent with creditworthiness being priced into rates
  • Grade and subgrade, LendingClub's proprietary risk assignments, were excluded by design. This model predicts rates using only information a borrower or external analyst would have at application time, making it a realistic approximation of rate transparency from the outside
  • About half of rate variance is explained by publicly available origination data, while the remainder may reflect unobserved underwriting factors including proprietary subgrade assignments

Reading the chart

The bars below show how much each feature contributes to rate determination. Higher importance means the model's accuracy drops more when that feature is removed, indicating stronger influence on pricing.

Features like grade and subgrade, which would otherwise dominate rate determination, are intentionally absent. What remains reflects the publicly observable signal in rate pricing.

A relatively stable rate structure can support portfolio analysis by suggesting that yield differences are related to measurable risk drivers rather than purely random variation.
